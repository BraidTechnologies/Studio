"In the age of AI, employers should address skill gaps and governments should support workers in upgrading their skills. McKinsey predicts that by 2030, automation could affect up to 30% of work hours, resulting in job transitions instead of mass unemployment. Sectors like healthcare and clean energy may experience increased job demand. Executives worry about skill shortages due to automation. Social and emotional skills will be in demand alongside new technologies. Wage implications are significant, with higher-wage jobs in demand and a risk of job loss in lower-wage positions. Policymakers should prioritize human capital and worker retraining to avoid a divided job market and promote both productivity growth and shared prosperity."
